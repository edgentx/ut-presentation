The Transition Challenge

With 8,500+ students across 11 majors, the Cockrell School is big. Entering as a first-year is exciting, but it can also be overwhelming.

The Cockrell School has built an ecosystem of support. The key is making sure every student knows about it and uses it early.

Where to Turn

Pre-Arrival

Start Before Day One

The transition begins before classes start. Connecting with students early sets the tone for their entire Cockrell experience.

Summer

Summer Bridge Program

Academic review, mock exams, and engineering design projects. Special focus on Calculus I preparation to build confidence before classes begin.

Orientation

New Student Orientation

Required for engineering students. Meet fellow students, explore campus, and learn about ESS support systems.

Welcome

Gone to Engineering

Held the night before the fall semester. Speakers, dinner, and a student organization fair featuring 70+ engineering groups.

Community

First-Year Interest Groups

FIGs place 18-25 students in a cohort taking 2-4 classes together, led by a peer mentor. A small community within the big university.

Academics

Academic Support

The foundation for success. Students who connect to support early are far more likely to thrive and complete their degree in four years.

Advising

ESS Advisors

Academic planning, adds/drops, major changes, and degree audits. Available by appointment and walk-in hours.

Learning Community

Ramshorn Scholars

First- and second-year learning community dedicated to helping undergrads transition to college-level engineering.

Tutoring

Engineering Study Tables

Drop-in tutoring in the new Engineering Education and Research Center (EER). No appointment needed.

Support

EOE Tutoring Program

Free one-on-one tutoring, up to 2 hours per student. Available campus-wide through EOE.

Housing

E3 Living Learning Community

Engineering housing in Jester West with weekly seminars, discussions, and transition activities built into residence life.

Belonging

Community and Belonging

Academic support alone is not enough. Students need to feel like they belong. The Cockrell School is the #5 producer of minority BS engineering graduates and #14 for women nationally.

Since 1970

Equal Opportunity in Engineering

EOE promotes recruitment and academic development of students from all backgrounds. Peer networks, mentoring, FIGs, and Lunch & Learns with corporate partners.

Since 1991

Women in Engineering Program

WEP connects students through recruitment initiatives, supportive structures, and community that promotes the success and advancement of women in engineering.

80+ Orgs

Engineering Student Life

ESL connects students with 80+ organizations, leadership development through LeaderShape, the Engineering Ambassadors program, and team-building events.

Wellness

Whole-Student Support

Campus fitness centers, health services, the Humanitarian Engineering Program, and study abroad opportunities. Supporting the whole student, not just the transcript.

"A student who has one person they trust and one group where they belong is far more likely to persist."

Careers

Career Readiness

90%+ of Cockrell graduates land a job or enter grad school. The average starting salary is $87,000. But career readiness should start early, not in senior year.

Careers

Engineering Career Assistance Center

ECAC provides resume help, career counseling, job search tools, and workshops. Connecting students early gives them time to explore and build experience.

Industry

Industry Partnerships

Mentoring, recruiting, and networking events through ESSC. Corporate Lunch & Learns for career exploration and professional development.

Hands-On

Experiential Learning

90% of students participate in internships or research. Texas Inventionworks, Create-a-Thon, and Projects for International Communities (PUC).

"We are developing student leaders that will become the CEOs, startup founders, inventors, and educators of the next generation."

Cockrell School of Engineering
My Approach

Bringing It All Together

My experience as a professor, a program liaison, and someone who has navigated complex systems on behalf of others has prepared me to do exactly this.

01

Early Connection

Make sure every student knows what resources exist before they need them. Start with Summer Bridge. Continue through Gone to Engineering. Follow up before the first exam.

02

Proactive Outreach

Use data and early alerts to identify students who may be struggling and reach out before they fall behind. Connect them to tutoring, advising, and FIGs.

03

Personal Touch

Every student's path is different. Listen first, then connect them with the right resource. A name, not a number. A person in their corner, not just a website link.
